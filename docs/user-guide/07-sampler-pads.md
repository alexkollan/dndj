# 7. Sampler Pads

[← Prev: Playlists](./06-playlists.md) · [Back to User Guide](./README.md) · [Next: Scenes →](./08-scenes.md)

---

The **SFX PADS** strip along the bottom holds **eight one-shot pads** for instant
sound effects — a sword clash, a thunderclap, a door slam, a dragon roar. Unlike
the decks, pads are built for *firing*, not mixing: every press plays the sound
from the top, and multiple presses overlap.

## Assigning a sound to a pad

**Drag a track** from the library onto any pad. The pad lights up and shows the
track name. That's it.

## Firing a pad

- **Click** the pad, or
- Press its **number key** — pads are mapped to **`1`–`8`** left to right.

Each fire is independent: hammer a pad and you'll get several overlapping copies
(great for crowd noise, rapid gunfire, etc.). The decks and other pads keep
playing undisturbed.

## Pad controls

- **■ (stop)** on the pad — stop any copies of that pad's sound currently playing.
- **Hover** a pad to reveal a small **volume slider** for that pad.
- **Right-click** a pad to **clear** it (unassign the sound).

## Stopping everything

The **■ STOP** button in the top bar (or **`Shift`+`S`**) stops all sampler
voices along with the decks.

## Good to know

- Pad assignments are **remembered** between sessions — your board comes back the
  way you left it.
- Pads are also **captured in Scenes**, so recalling a scene restores which sounds
  were on which pads. See [Scenes](./08-scenes.md).
- Because pads are one-shots, they ignore looping — they always play once per
  trigger.
